Afang Soup

Afang soup is one of the most traditionaal Nigerian soups. It is very nutritious, medicinal and native to the Efik people of Nigeria. It is very delicious so let's get to the preparation of my favorite meal!!!

Ingredients:

Items Measurement
Assorted Meats 1kg
Water Leaves 4 bunches
Okazi/Afang Leaves 2-3 cups
Palm Oil 2-3 Cooking Spoons
Smoked Fish 1-2 Pieces
Stockfish 3 Pieces
Periwinkles(shelled or deshelled) 2 Cups
Scotch Bonnet Pepper 1-2 Tablespoons
Crayfish 1 Tablespoon
Knorr Chicken Cubes 2 Cubes
Maggi Crayfish 1 Cube
Salt As preferred/to taste

Preparation:

  1. Season and boil the meats on high heat, starting with tougher meats first. (Offals can be boiled seperately if they are bloody).
  2. When the meats are almost soft, add the smoked fish and stockfish and continue to boil until very soft.
  3. While the meats are cooking, get the leaves ready.
  4. Slice and wash the water leaves to remove all traces of dirt
  5. Also, wash and soak the Okazi/Afang leaves in hot water for 2 minutes. This process will help soften the Afang leaves
  6. Transfer the Afang leaves into a blender, add a small amount of water and blend to preferred consistency. The leaves could also be pounded or grinded.
  7. Soak the smoked catfish in hot water, clean to remove traces of dirt, shred and then set aside.
  8. To the meat stock, add the Crayfish, blended pepper, Smoked Prawns, Smoked Catfish, Maggi Crayfish and Periwinkles if you're using any. Turn the heat down to medium.
  9. Leave to cook for 3-5 minutes
  10. Now add the washed water leaves, stir and combine and leave to cook for 2-3 minutes. The water leaves will wilt in the soup as it's got quite a lot of water contents, do not be tempted to add too much.
  11. Now add the palm oil, stir and combine, leave to cook for 3 minutes.
  12. Add the blended Okazi/ Afang leaves, stir and combine, leave to cook for 5 minutes or until the leaves are tender enough for you.
  13. Taste for seasoning at this time and adjust if necessary.
  14. Switch the heat off and let it simmer with the residual heat for another minute or two.
  15. And it's ready.

Enjoy with any swallow of your choice.
